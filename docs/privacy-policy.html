Privacy Policy

Niduna Currency Converter — last updated 12 June 2026

In short: the app has no accounts, no sign-in, no analytics, and no tracking by us. Your settings and favorites stay on your device. The free version shows non-personalized ads through Google AdMob.

Who we are

Niduna Currency Converter is an independent app published by Luis Charro. Contact: luisjchg@gmail.com.

Data the app stores

Everything the app saves — your preferred currencies, favorites, decimal settings, theme choice, cached exchange rates and chart history, and any premium unlocks — is stored locally on your device only. We run no servers, require no account, and never receive a copy of this data. Uninstalling the app deletes it.

Exchange-rate providers

To show conversions and charts, the app requests publicly available exchange-rate data over HTTPS from Frankfurter (European Central Bank fiat rates) and the open fawazahmed0/exchange-api dataset (crypto rates). These requests contain no account information or identifiers added by us; like any web request, the provider technically receives your IP address.

Advertising (free version)

The free version shows banner and optional rewarded ads through Google AdMob. The app explicitly requests non-personalized ads. AdMob may still collect and process certain data to serve and measure ads (such as IP address, device information, and coarse ad-performance data). See Google's Privacy Policy and how Google uses information from AdMob apps. Purchasing "Remove Ads" disables all advertising.

Purchases

Premium unlocks are one-time purchases processed entirely by Google Play. We receive no payment details. Entitlements are stored on your device.

Children

The app is a general-audience utility and does not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children.
